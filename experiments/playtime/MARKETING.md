Catalogued concrete resources across design press, Apple, publishing, and parent communities
Concrete pointers per category. Treat these as starting points to investigate, not a finished plan — some publications shift in relevance and a few I'm naming from memory might be less active than they were.

For design press, the most natural targets are It's Nice That, Creative Boom, The Creative Independent (which does long-form interviews with creators and is very on-brand for you), Eye on Design (AIGA's publication), Colossal, and Kottke.org. Sidebar.io is a curated daily design link feed and getting one Mipolai post into rotation there would matter. In the Netherlands specifically, Dezeen and Dutch Design Week pay attention to indie work, and "ad-free Dutch dad in a custom physics engine" lands naturally in that frame. How to pitch them: don't write a press release. Email the specific writer whose work you actually like, reference something they wrote, and include one short paragraph plus one screenshot or short video plus the App Store link. Three minutes of reading. Most pitches arrive as 1500 words and a PDF; the short personal pitch wins disproportionately.

For Apple's editorial team, the official channel is the "App Store Promotion Request" form accessible through App Store Connect. They want a timely angle, ideally exclusivity (an update launching alongside the feature), strong design, and a clear story. Lead time is typically 6–8 weeks before you want the feature to land. Also submit for the Apple Design Awards annually — even being shortlisted gets significant visibility, and your aesthetic plus the no-ads/no-IAP commitment is exactly the kind of thing Apple uses for "the App Store at its best" framing.

For the picture-book and illustration world, look at Picturebook Makers (run by dPICTUS, interviews with illustrators, perfectly on-brand), The Marginalian (Maria Popova, formerly Brain Pickings), Mr Schu Reads, The Horn Book, and 100 Scope Notes for school librarians. Indie publishers worth knowing for tone reference and possible partnerships: Enchanted Lion Books, Flying Eye Books, Cicada Books, and in your region Querido and Lemniscaat. The Bologna Children's Book Fair is the annual gathering of this whole world — even attending as a visitor would put you in front of the right people. On Instagram, the hashtags #picturebookmakers and #kidlitart will surface the community.

For anti-slop parent communities, the institutional ones are Common Sense Media, Children's Technology Review, and Smart Apps for Kids — being well-rated there compounds in App Store search results and in parent Google searches. On Substack, Anne Helen Petersen's Culture Study has covered kids' media to a huge audience, Anya Kamenetz writes thoughtfully about kids and tech, and Jessica Grose at the NYT has the right ecosystem. On Reddit, r/iPadKids is small but specifically curated, r/Parenting is much larger but less targeted, and the screen-skeptical adjacent communities (r/MinimalistParenting, r/ScreenFreeParenting) are worth lurking in even if they're not quite the right buyers.

For process content, the obvious channels are YouTube and Instagram, but a less obvious one for your specific aesthetic is Are.na. It's where designers, illustrators, and creative-coding people collect their references. Starting an Are.na channel — "Things in the workshop" or similar — with cropped screenshots, sketches, half-frames of Mipo animations is exactly the surface that the design-press-reading audience scrolls. Vimeo over YouTube for short polished videos; YouTube for the longer explainers; TikTok is a worse fit than Instagram or Mastodon for what you make.

One principle worth holding through all of this: depth beats breadth. One feature in The Creative Independent matters more than fifty generic listicle mentions. One Instagram post from Oliver Jeffers noticing your work matters more than a year of paid ads. Spend the time finding and engaging with ten specific people, not broadcasting to ten thousand strangers. At your current scale, ten right people compounds into something real within months; ten thousand wrong impressions stays at zero.
