1) Lore-at-a-glance (4 short paragraphs)
The Mipo World feels like someone doodled a physics textbook in a 1970-s children’s encyclopedia, then blew life into the margins. Everything is hand-drawn and slightly rubbery: clouds bounce, shadows wiggle, and “gravity” is more of a polite suggestion than a rule. Mipos—semi-sentient, potato-head collage-creatures—are the stars of this squishy pocket-universe. They come apart, snap together, stretch, repaint, or sprout new gadgets at the player’s whim, and each new combination might unlock a fresh ability or a slapstick disaster.

Across seven headline regions the tone stays playful: MipoTown’s toaster-houses and dancing doors act as the social hub; The Playground is a build-your-own Rube-Goldberg of trampolines and cannons; the Forest of Limbs is a wardrobe made of living body parts; Wobbly Hills turn walking into bowling; the Sky-Sketch Isles host toast-gliders and spaghetti-copters; the newly unearthed Burrow Below is a tinkerer’s maze of scrap; and Busybody Bay parodies grown-up jobs with earnest nonsense (the sandwich-elevator clock-tower, the trampoline-inspector’s office, etc.).

Mipos themselves are open-hearted slapstick inventors. Their emotions broadcast physically—eyes balloon when startled, limbs pop off when annoyed, whole bodies bounce with joy. They spend their days glue-crafting improbable machines, bartering poems for flying fish, and trying (often failing) to keep their own parts attached. Their myths are equally silly: a giggle-powered sun, a sleepy moon that sneezed the world into being, and a legendary Big Box toy store where perfect Mipos are displayed forever.

The world’s design philosophy is “serious nonsense”: gentle satire, physics that favour fun over realism, and a collage aesthetic that invites endless player creativity—build it, break it, laugh, and remix. Underneath the chaos are clear themes: modular identity, expressive emotion, discovery through tinkering, and cooperation born of comedic failure.

✨ Aspect-Chain “Bare Vowel” Grammar ✨
(the i a o system we just chose)

Particle	Core idea	Typical uses	Quick mnemonic
i	STATE / BE – something simply is	identity, description, location, possession, emotion that is already present	high, steady vowel ⇒ stable
a	BECOME / MOVE / BEGIN – entering a new state or heading toward a goal	start, change, motion, inchoative (“get-”, “become-”)	mouth opens as you “step forward”
o	DO / ACT / AFFECT – deliberate, ongoing action	create, use, manipulate, perform, experience actively	round, forceful sound ⇒ energy
(optional) u	WITH / TOGETHER / BY-MEANS-OF (comitative / instrumental)	companionship or tool marker	lips meet → “together”

Position.
Place the particle right after the subject (or topic) and before the main predicate chunk.
You can chain several particles inside one clause for layered meaning.

mi  i  momo           I am (full of) love.

mi  i  mokI  o  di  widi
I (in a state of) wanting → do eat ice-cream.
Other handy bits

Reduplication intensifies or makes something iterative (miso miso = sob hard / keep sobbing).

Paragrammar consonants (m, t, h, …) can be whispered after the clause for extra mood.

Word order is still loose S-V-O, but the aspect vowel anchors the clause core.

Negation slots right before the aspect particle: mi NA i lala → “I am not good.”

🛠️ Building your own
Choose the root(s).
HAFA = jump, MOMO = love, KOKA = box …

Pick the aspect.
i if it simply is, a if it’s about to happen/become, o if someone is making it happen.

Add extras.
u companion/tool, reduplication for intensity, paragrammar consonant for tone.

Template:
(Subject) (NEG?) (Aspect vowel) (Predicate roots) (u + instrument/companion) (location/time) (paragram-consonant)

Play with it, sing it, mash roots together — the chain is flexible but stays crystal clear once your ear catches the three key vowels. When you need more nuance (tense, evidential, etc.) we can still layer little particles after the vowel without breaking the core rhythm.
